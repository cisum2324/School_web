Brigada Eskwela 2024 is a nationwide initiative aimed at preparing public schools for the upcoming school year. It encourages the whole community to join efforts in cleaning, repairing, and improving school facilities. Teachers, students, parents, and even local government units are encouraged to volunteer for the event. This year, the theme focuses on fostering collaboration and promoting the importance of education in nation-building. Volunteers help by painting classrooms, fixing furniture, and clearing school grounds. Educational materials are also organized and prepared to ensure that students have everything they need for the first day of class. The program has a strong emphasis on environmental sustainability by promoting eco-friendly practices like waste segregation and tree planting. Through Brigada Eskwela, the Department of Education aims to instill the value of bayanihan or communal unity in the minds of the Filipino youth. The event also strengthens the partnership between the school, parents, and the local community. Finally, Brigada Eskwela 2024 serves as a reminder that preparing the learning environment is essential for fostering the future of Filipino students.

Oplan Balik Eskwela 2024 is a government-led initiative to ensure that students are ready and able to attend school at the beginning of the school year. It aims to address any challenges that families may face in preparing their children for school. The program coordinates with different government agencies, such as the Department of Education (DepEd), local government units (LGUs), and various non-governmental organizations (NGOs). Volunteers assist in providing school supplies, uniforms, and other essential materials to students in need. Oplan Balik Eskwela also offers free medical check-ups to ensure students are healthy before returning to school. It encourages community participation to create an organized and supportive environment for students. The program includes setting up help desks in schools and government offices to assist parents with enrollment and school requirements. In addition, it provides guidance on how to follow safety protocols, especially in the context of the pandemic. Through Oplan Balik Eskwela, the government seeks to reduce barriers to education, making it easier for every child to attend school. The initiative emphasizes that a successful school year starts with full community support and preparation.

Nutrition Month 2024 in the Philippines will be observed throughout the month of July, with the theme **"Sa Pagkain ng Wasto at Tamang Ehersisyo, iwas sakit, tayo’y magkaisa!"** (In Proper Nutrition and Exercise, Let’s Unite to Prevent Illness!). The celebration aims to raise awareness about the importance of proper nutrition and an active lifestyle in maintaining good health. Schools, communities, and government agencies organize activities such as health seminars, cooking demonstrations, fitness challenges, and nutrition education campaigns. It also encourages individuals to make healthier food choices and engage in regular physical activities to combat malnutrition, obesity, and other lifestyle-related diseases. In 2024, the focus will be on the importance of combining balanced diets with consistent exercise to promote overall well-being. Nutrition Month is an opportunity to promote nutrition literacy among the youth, especially in schools, to help students understand the value of eating nutritious meals. Additionally, it highlights the role of local foods in achieving a healthy and balanced diet. Various food companies and nutrition advocates also participate in spreading helpful nutrition information to the public. Through Nutrition Month, the government and organizations hope to inspire positive changes in dietary habits across the country. The celebration ultimately promotes healthier communities for the future of the nation.

Buwan ng Wika 2024 (Language Month 2024) will be celebrated in the Philippines throughout the month of August, with the theme **"Wikang Filipino: Kasangkapan sa Pagpapayaman ng Kultura at Pagkakaisa"** (Filipino Language: Tool for Enriching Culture and Unity). This annual celebration emphasizes the importance of promoting the Filipino language as a tool for preserving and enriching Filipino culture and fostering unity among Filipinos, regardless of region or dialect. During Buwan ng Wika, various activities are held in schools, communities, and government offices, such as cultural presentations, poetry and essay writing contests, theater performances, and language workshops. These activities encourage the use of Filipino in daily communication and highlight its role in the nation’s identity and progress. The month-long event also includes promoting the appreciation of the different Philippine languages and dialects, emphasizing their value in enriching the diverse culture of the country. In 2024, the celebration aims to inspire the youth to embrace their native language and take pride in their cultural heritage. Schools often hold storytelling sessions, research competitions, and exhibits that showcase the history and evolution of the Filipino language. The focus is on strengthening the connection of the Filipino people through their shared language, fostering solidarity and mutual understanding. Through Buwan ng Wika, Filipinos are reminded that language is not only a medium of communication but also a key to preserving cultural traditions and ensuring a united future.

**History Month 2024** in the Philippines will be commemorated in September, focusing on celebrating the country's rich history and the significant events, figures, and movements that shaped its national identity. The theme for 2024 is **"Kasaysayan: Pag-alala at Pagtuturo ng mga Mahahalagang Pagyabong ng Bansa"** (History: Remembering and Teaching the Significant Growth of the Nation). The month-long celebration aims to honor the pivotal moments in Philippine history and foster a deeper understanding of the struggles and triumphs that led to the nation's independence, freedom, and democratic values. During History Month, activities such as lectures, discussions, and exhibits about important historical events are conducted in schools, universities, and government institutions. Historical documentaries and films about national heroes, revolutionaries, and milestones like the Philippine Revolution, the Declaration of Independence, and World War II are often shown to the public. These activities serve as a reminder of the sacrifices made by past generations and the importance of preserving history for future generations. History Month 2024 will also spotlight the contributions of unsung heroes and ordinary Filipinos whose actions were pivotal to the nation's development. It is a time to reflect on the country’s challenges and achievements, and to strengthen the Filipino people's collective consciousness about their shared past. Throughout the celebration, there will be emphasis on the role of youth in learning about and preserving Philippine history, ensuring that the lessons of the past continue to guide the nation forward. Special programs may also include interactive museum tours, history quizzes, and public forums with historians and cultural experts. The ultimate goal is to inspire pride in the Filipino heritage and to educate the public on how history informs the present and shapes the future.

**English Month 2024** in the Philippines will be celebrated throughout the month of November, with a focus on promoting the importance of the English language in communication, education, and global interactions. The theme for 2024 is **"Empowering Communication: Building Bridges Through the English Language"**. This theme emphasizes the role of English in fostering better understanding, collaboration, and connections both within the country and internationally. During English Month, schools, universities, and other institutions organize a variety of activities aimed at improving language proficiency and appreciation for English. These include speech competitions, storytelling contests, debate tournaments, and writing workshops, all designed to encourage students to express themselves confidently in English. Educational seminars and webinars on effective communication skills may also be conducted to enhance public speaking, writing, and reading comprehension. The celebration highlights the significance of English not only as a medium of instruction but also as a tool for professional growth and international collaboration. It is an opportunity to recognize the achievements of individuals who have excelled in using English in fields such as literature, journalism, business, and diplomacy. English Month 2024 will also focus on the integration of creative arts, with activities such as poetry readings, drama performances, and English language film screenings. These events aim to foster a deeper appreciation for English literature and the arts, while encouraging participants to explore the cultural diversity and richness of the English-speaking world. Ultimately, English Month serves as a reminder of the crucial role that language plays in building bridges across cultures and ensuring effective communication in an increasingly globalized world. It is also an opportunity to inspire students and professionals alike to continuously improve their English language skills for personal and professional success.

Kinder

Kinder, or Kindergarten, is the first formal year of education for young children, typically around ages 5 to 6. It serves as an introduction to school, helping children adjust to structured learning environments. In Kinder, children begin to develop foundational skills such as early literacy, numeracy, and social interaction. Teachers focus on teaching basic concepts like recognizing letters, numbers, shapes, and colors, as well as fostering communication and motor skills. The goal is to create a nurturing and engaging atmosphere that prepares children for the transition to higher grades, both academically and socially.

Grade 1

Grade 1 is a foundational year where students begin their journey in formal education. In this grade, the focus is on building basic literacy and numeracy skills, such as recognizing letters, learning to read, and developing writing abilities. Students also start to understand simple math concepts like addition, subtraction, and number sequencing. The classroom environment is designed to foster a love for learning through interactive lessons and hands-on activities. By the end of Grade 1, students should be able to read simple sentences and perform basic arithmetic operations

Grade 2

Grade 2 expands on the skills learned in Grade 1 by introducing more complex math concepts and deeper reading comprehension exercises. In math, students begin to explore addition and subtraction with larger numbers, as well as the concept of place value. They also develop stronger reading skills, learning to read longer sentences and paragraphs, with an emphasis on understanding the meaning of texts. Writing becomes more structured, with students learning to write simple sentences and paragraphs. This grade emphasizes building confidence in both math and language arts, preparing students for more advanced concepts in the future.

Grade 3

In Grade 3, students focus on strengthening both their math and language arts skills. Math instruction becomes more challenging with the introduction of multiplication and division, along with the continued practice of addition and subtraction. Language arts instruction includes reading comprehension strategies, vocabulary building, and the development of writing skills, including simple essays. This year also marks the introduction of more detailed literature, where students begin to analyze characters, plot, and themes. By the end of Grade 3, students are expected to write coherent paragraphs and solve basic word problems in math.

Grade 4

Grade 4 brings a greater emphasis on critical thinking and problem-solving in both math and other subjects. In math, students explore fractions, learn about basic geometry, and begin working with multi-step word problems. Science and social studies become more integrated into the curriculum, with students studying topics like ecosystems, the water cycle, and historical events. Writing instruction is expanded to include writing reports and explanations of scientific concepts. Overall, Grade 4 is a time for students to build upon their previous knowledge and apply it to more complex subjects.

Grade 5

: Grade 5 is a transition year where students tackle more advanced topics in both math and language arts. Math instruction covers decimal operations, percentages, and basic data analysis, while students continue to practice multi-step word problems. In language arts, students refine their writing by working on essays, stories, and persuasive texts, with an emphasis on grammar and sentence structure. The curriculum also expands to include in-depth studies of science and history, preparing students for the more specialized subjects in middle school. By the end of Grade 5, students are expected to have a strong grasp of mathematical concepts and be able to write more detailed and organized compositions.

Grade 6

Grade 6 prepares students for the academic challenges of junior high by introducing more advanced topics in math, writing, and critical thinking. In math, students delve into concepts such as ratios, percentages, and algebraic thinking. Writing instruction focuses on crafting longer, more complex essays, research reports, and creative writing projects. Science and social studies lessons become more focused on analytical thinking, where students explore topics like the history of civilizations and scientific investigations. This grade emphasizes self-discipline and responsibility as students work on larger projects, helping them build the skills needed for success in higher education.

Accreditation and Affiliation of Maharlika Integrated School

Ang Maharlika Integrated School ay nakatanggap ng iba’t ibang mga akreditasyon at afiliasyon mula sa mga kilalang organisasyon sa larangan ng edukasyon. Ito ay patunay ng dedikasyon ng paaralan sa pagbibigay ng mataas na kalidad ng edukasyon at ang pagsunod nito sa mga pambansang pamantayan.

ACCREDITATION:

  • DepEd Accreditation: Ang Maharlika Integrated School ay isang akreditadong paaralan ng Department of Education (DepEd), na nagsisiguro ng pagsunod sa mga pambansang patakaran at kurikulum sa edukasyon.
  • ISO Certification: Ang paaralan ay may ISO certification para sa kalidad ng pamamahala at operational processes nito, na nagpapakita ng pagiging epektibo sa pagpapalakad ng institusyon.
  • Philippine Accrediting Association of Schools, Colleges, and Universities (PAASCU): Ang Maharlika Integrated School ay miyembro ng PAASCU, isang prestihiyosong ahensya na nag-audit at nagpapatibay ng kalidad ng mga institusyon ng edukasyon sa Pilipinas.

AFFILIATION:

  • Philippine Business for Social Progress (PBSP): Ang paaralan ay nakipag-ugnayan sa PBSP upang magsagawa ng mga proyektong may kinalaman sa pangangalaga sa kapaligiran at pagbibigay ng mga serbisyo sa komunidad.
  • Philippine National School Sports Association (PNSSA): Kasapi ang Maharlika Integrated School sa PNSSA, kung saan nagsasagawa ng mga kompetisyon sa sports at iba pang extra-curricular activities.
  • Association of Private Educators of the Philippines (APEP): Ang paaralan ay kasapi ng APEP, isang grupo ng mga pribadong paaralan na nagsusulong ng mga makabago at epektibong pamamaraan sa pagtuturo.

Ang mga akreditasyon at afiliasyong ito ay nagpapakita ng dedikasyon ng Maharlika Integrated School na magbigay ng mataas na kalidad na edukasyon at palakasin ang ugnayan nito sa iba pang mga institusyon at organisasyon na may layuning itaguyod ang kabutihang pang-edukasyon.

Community Involvement and Outreach Programs

Maharlika Integrated School strongly believes in the importance of community engagement and social responsibility. Our outreach programs aim to develop the values of empathy, cooperation, and service among our students, while positively impacting the communities we serve.

Our Key Outreach Programs:

  • Annual Feeding Program: Every year, we organize a feeding program for the less fortunate children in our neighboring communities. This initiative provides meals to hundreds of children, fostering a sense of compassion and volunteerism among our students and faculty.
  • Environmental Awareness Campaign: Our students actively participate in various environmental campaigns, including tree planting activities, waste management seminars, and clean-up drives in nearby areas, promoting environmental sustainability and awareness.
  • Health and Wellness Outreach: In partnership with local health organizations, we conduct free health check-up events, medical missions, and health education seminars for the community, emphasizing the importance of health and wellness in everyday life.
  • Educational Assistance Program: We provide educational support to underprivileged students, offering free tutoring, school supplies, and scholarship opportunities to deserving learners, helping bridge the gap in educational access.
  • Community Arts and Culture Projects: Our students and faculty collaborate in various art and cultural programs that showcase the talents of the local community, fostering creativity and cultural appreciation.

These programs are a testament to our school’s commitment to developing socially responsible individuals who understand the importance of giving back to the community. Together, we can make a difference and inspire positive change.
